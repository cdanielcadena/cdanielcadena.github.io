Masters Students

Sebastián Martínez

Masters Student

js.martinez1234[at]uniandes.edu.co

My research interests center on evolutionary ecology and species delimitation within an integrative taxonomy framework, supported by ecophysiological approaches, with a particular emphasis on the thermal ecology of Neotropical organisms. I am interested in understanding how genetic patterns and physiological traits shape intraspecific variation, and how these traits influence species’ capacity to persist and adapt across environmental gradients. To address these questions, I employ an integrative computational biology approach that combines biostatistics, genomic data analyses, and Geographic Information Systems (GIS).

María Elisa Mendiwelso

Masters Student

me.mendiwelso[at]uniandes.edu.co

My research lies at the intersection of natural history, ecology, evolutionary biology, and computational biology, with a particular focus on Neotropical birds. I am especially interested in understanding how ecological traits, life-history strategies, and environmental conditions influence contaminant exposure and accumulation, and how these processes have changed over time. I also use quantitative, genetic, and genomic approaches, including genome assembly and demographic inference, to investigate the evolutionary history, adaptation, and diversification of species.

Juan Daniel Rodríguez

Masters Student

j.rodriguez94@uniandes.edu.co

My research focuses on the evolutionary processes that generate and maintain biological diversity. I am particularly interested in how geological history and environmental pressures interact to drive adaptation and the evolution of ecological and behavioral traits in tropical organisms. By integrating genomics, phylogenomics, bioinformatics, and ecology, I aim to identify the mechanisms underlying adaptation, trait evolution, and diversification, and to better understand the evolutionary history of tropical biodiversity.

Undergraduate Students

Catalina Montoya

Undergraduate Student

c.montoyab@uniandes.edu.co

My research focuses on the genomic basis of phenotypic evolution. I am particularly interested in understanding the genetic changes associated with the loss of flight in steamer ducks (Tachyeres) from southern South America using comparative genomics. I also contribute to an interdisciplinary project that brings together scientists, local communities, and other stakeholders to advance the development and implementation of solutions to the challenges posed by invasive hippopotamus populations in Colombia.
